Build Virtual World

Building Virtual Worlds (BVW) is a project focused course in ETC. We build five games with different platforms - Kinect, Oculus Rift, Leap Motion, Eye Gazer and PS Move in group of five(two programmers, two artists, one sound designer).

My role for BVW is a programmer. At the end of the course, the best worlds were showcased at a festival where industry professionals come to experience our worlds. Two of my five games made into the festival(17 out of 75). All games developed during BVW uses the Unity Engine.


Harsh Light

Platform: Eye Gazer | Duration: 1 week | Role: Programmer

Contributions: controller for all traps, interfaces for generating the trap, score system and score board.

Harsh Light is a 2D side scroller avoidance game using the Eye Gazer. Guests can control a spotlight that the fairy flies to with only eye movements to guide a fairy avoid a variety of traps, escape from the darkness, and try to beat the high score.

The most interesting thing about this game is that everyone who is watching thinks it is easy and they can do better. But when they are playing, they involuntarily look at those traps come from lights which they have to avoid.


Muse Meow

Platform: PS Move | Duration: 3 weeks | Role: Programmer

Contributions: event controller for the whole story, auto-checkpoints for each step.

Muse Meow is about a happy cat lives in 'The Scream' who jumps out of the painting one day as a dog is chasing him. Guests should solve the puzzles and help him go through HomoVitruvianus, The Persistence of Memory, Mona Lisa, The Shrimps, Last Supper, escape from the dog, and go back to his original painting!

To explore more possibilities, we brought alive background music that can tell the story depend on how guests are playing.


Summoning Santa

Platform: Makey Makey and a 55-inch pyramid hologram built by ourselves | Duration: 3 weeks | Role: Programmer

Contributions: camera controller that transfers the initial camera outputs to four-sides output for hologram in real time, DMX light controller, Makey Makey gingerbread voodoo cookies.

In this interactive play, guests are invited into the room and see a ghostly apparition of Santa Claus in the hologram pyramid and hear him calling for help. With Santa's instruction, the guests perform the summoning ritual which involves pouring milk, serving cookies, ringing a bell, lighting a candle, and tracing a heptagram in a spellbook. After performing the ritual, the guests realize that they have actually summoned the devious Krampus. Sensing trouble, the real Santa emerges from the fireplace. Then Santa instructs the guests to try different ways may help defeat the monster such as to stab gingerbread voodoo cookies. Finally the guests can defeat the Krampus, then Santa gives the guests candy canes and wishes them a Merry Christmas.


Haiku

Platform: Oculus and Leap Motion | Duration: 2 weeks | Role: Programmer

Contributions: Leap Motion handler, event trigger, ending.

In Haiku, guests can walk through an abstract environment based on Japanese Ukiyo-e art. As they follow the path of a quiet stream they encounter abstract sculptures floating in space. Viewing these sculptures from the proper angle causes the shapes to align in perspective, creating a concrete image. Each image is accompanied by a line from a Masaoka Shiki haiku.


Roach Rush

Platform: Kinect | Duration: 2 weeks | Role: Programmer

Contributions: Kinect handler, game play, cockroach controller.

Roach Rush is a Kinect game where guests would protect their girlfriend by killing all cockroaches around her.
